Project Overview

The Mongolian NREN visibility project aimed to elevate the national and regional presence of Mongolia's National Research and Education Network (Erdemnet) by addressing critical visibility gaps and promoting sustainable collaboration models. Through strategic events, comprehensive surveys, dedicated online platforms, and extensive stakeholder engagement, the project successfully fostered cross-border partnerships and identified clear future directions for Mongolian academic connectivity.

Project Objectives

Primary Goal

Increase the visibility and operational capacity of Mongolia's NREN, Erdemnet, nationally and regionally, thereby supporting its sustainable growth and integration into global academic networks.

Institutional Needs Assessment

Conduct comprehensive analysis to identify institutional requirements and potential for NREN usage across Mongolia's academic landscape.

Capacity Building

Build organizational and technical capacity through strategic knowledge sharing initiatives and international collaboration.

Strategic Roadmap

Create a comprehensive roadmap for Erdemnet's future expansion, sustainability, and integration with regional networks.

Regional Integration

Foster connections with regional NRENs to establish Mongolia as an active participant in the Asian research network ecosystem.

Policy Advocacy

Engage government stakeholders to advocate for supportive policies and licensing frameworks for NREN operations.

Challenges & Gaps

ISP License Limitation

Erdemnet still lacks essential ISP licensing which significantly hinders its ability to scale connectivity across universities and expand its service offerings.

Resource Constraints

Erdemnet's technical staff and financial capacity are severely strained due to its primary focus on secondary schools, limiting university-level services.

Leadership Awareness Gap

Some university leaders lack comprehensive understanding of NREN/TEIN value proposition, reducing institutional uptake and strategic support.

Technical Connection Barriers

Institutions raised significant concerns about complex procedures, connection costs, and training requirements for connecting to Erdemnet infrastructure.

Future Directions

ISP License Restoration

Continue strategic advocacy with CRC and MEDS to formally request license reinstatement for Erdemnet, enabling expanded service capabilities.

University Leadership Engagement

Implement targeted outreach campaigns to senior university management to ensure institutional buy-in for TEIN connectivity and strategic adoption.

International Collaboration Expansion

Develop sector-specific pilot projects with Korean, Philippine, and Japanese NREN partners to demonstrate tangible research collaboration benefits.

Technical Capacity Building

Conduct regular comprehensive training workshops for local network engineers and researchers on NREN usage, maintenance, and optimization.

Platform Sustainability

Maintain and continuously update www.erdemnet.mn as a dynamic knowledge and support portal for the Mongolian research community.
